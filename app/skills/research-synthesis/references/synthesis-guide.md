# Synthesis Guide

## Source Credibility Framework
- **Tier 1**: Peer-reviewed journals, official government data, major institutions
- **Tier 2**: Industry reports, conference proceedings, reputable news outlets
- **Tier 3**: Expert blogs, company publications, pre-prints
- **Tier 4**: Opinion pieces, social media, unverified sources

## Evidence Strength Ratings
- **Strong**: Multiple Tier 1 sources agree, large sample sizes, replicated findings
- **Moderate**: Some Tier 1 support, but limited replication or smaller samples
- **Weak**: Only Tier 3-4 sources, anecdotal evidence, single studies
- **Insufficient**: No reliable evidence found

## Synthesis Structure
1. **Executive Summary**: 3-5 sentence overview for decision-makers
2. **Key Findings**: Top 3-7 most important insights with evidence strength
3. **Detailed Analysis**: Thematic grouping with source citations
4. **Consensus View**: Areas where most sources agree
5. **Debate Areas**: Topics with conflicting evidence
6. **Knowledge Gaps**: What we still don't know
7. **Recommendations**: Actionable next steps

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] All claims have cited sources
- [ ] Conflicting evidence is acknowledged
- [ ] Confidence levels are clearly stated
- [ ] Limitations section is included
- [ ] Executive summary can stand alone
