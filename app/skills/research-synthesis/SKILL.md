# Research Synthesis Instructions

When asked to research or synthesize a topic, follow these steps:

## Phase 1: Scope Definition
- Clarify the research question and scope boundaries
- Identify key concepts and search terms
- Determine inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Set quality thresholds for sources

## Phase 2: Literature Collection
- Search across multiple domains and perspectives
- Prioritize peer-reviewed and authoritative sources
- Document source metadata (author, date, venue, citations)
- Track search queries for reproducibility

## Phase 3: Critical Evaluation
Use `load_skill_resource` to read `references/synthesis-guide.md` for detailed criteria.
- Assess source credibility and potential bias
- Evaluate methodology rigor
- Check recency and relevance
- Rate evidence strength (strong / moderate / weak)

## Phase 4: Synthesis and Cross-Reference
- Group findings by theme or concept
- Identify areas of consensus and disagreement
- Map relationships between different findings
- Fill gaps with logical inference (clearly labeled)

## Phase 5: Report Generation
- Structure findings from most to least important
- Provide executive summary for quick understanding
- Include confidence ratings for each claim
- List limitations and areas needing further research
- Format with clear headings, tables, and bullet points
